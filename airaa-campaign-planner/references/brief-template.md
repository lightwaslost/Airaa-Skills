# Brief Template & Building Blocks

Reference this file during Phase 5 (Brief Generation) to ensure every section is complete and creator-ready.

---

## The 8 Building Blocks (Every Brief Needs All 8)

1. **Campaign Identity** — brand name, what they are, the hook story, platforms, budget/CPM
2. **Source Content** — exactly where creators get the content (URL, Drive link, asset bundle, or "make original")
3. **Content Style Guide** — what to make, length, energy, editing style, what NOT to make
4. **Hook & Caption Bank** — 5-10 opening lines + 3-5 caption templates written for this specific brand
5. **Audience Requirements** — Tier 1 %, geo targeting, engagement rate floor
6. **Mandatory Requirements** — all hard rules as a checklist (30 days live, submit within 30 min, etc.)
7. **Prohibited Content** — specific list — always include brand-specific items from Q&A, never just generic
8. **Submission & Payout** — step-by-step submission process, CPM, min views, max payout

Before finalizing, ask yourself: *"Does this brief tell a creator everything they need to hit publish today?"* Fill every gap.

---

## Platform-Specific Notes

**TikTok:** Native slideshow ≠ CapCut video export — always specify which is required. Algorithm heavily rewards Tier 1 audience. VPN + device setup required for non-US creators targeting US.

**Instagram Reels:** Audience screenshot showing Tier 1 % required. 40%+ is the floor. Tag brand handle in caption. Location tag helps for geo-specific campaigns.

**YouTube Shorts:** Screen recording of analytics required (not just a screenshot) for most campaigns. Max payout caps tend to be higher than TikTok/Instagram at same CPM.

**X (Twitter):** Lower reach, used mainly for tech/crypto/finance brands. Much smaller creator pool.
