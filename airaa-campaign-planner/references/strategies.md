## The 10 Archetypes

### 1. YouTube Funnel Machine
**Goal:** Drive YouTube subscribers  
**Use when:** Brand has a YouTube channel and wants short-form platforms to feed it  
**Key brief elements:** Dedicated page requirement, account creation guide, VPN/US targeting instructions, bio links to YouTube channel  
**Creator effort:** HIGH — must build dedicated fan accounts  
**CPM:** $0.75–$1.50  
**Real example:** Finance educators, lifestyle vloggers, educators with 10k–500k subs scaling to millions

---

### 2. Viral Moment Amplifier
**Goal:** Saturate social media around one specific moment  
**Use when:** Brand has a compelling single event — Shark Tank appearance, product launch, face reveal, album drop  
**Key brief elements:** Time-sensitive window, single source video URL, pre-written brand story hook, open creative format, no dedicated page needed  
**Creator effort:** LOW — any page, post on any account  
**CPM:** $0.50–$1.50  
**Real example:** ClipFarm/Bloom (Shark Tank episode), ChainsFR (faceless creator face reveal)

---

### 3. Logo Placement Network
**Goal:** Brand awareness at scale in a specific geography  
**Use when:** Brand needs impressions in a specific country/market at low cost; common for betting/gaming brands  
**Key brief elements:** PNG logo file (transparent background), geo requirement, content type (sports/entertainment), strict prohibited content list  
**Creator effort:** VERY LOW — any sports/entertainment page, just overlay the logo  
**CPM:** $0.30–$1.00  
**Real example:** Betway (Africa), Northstar Bets (Canada)

---

### 4. Music Promotion Push
**Goal:** Drive audio streams, chart position, social following for an artist  
**Sub-model A (audio attach):** Creators use official sound in any content they make. Very open.  
**Sub-model B (artist clipping):** Creators clip artist's existing content and drive to YouTube/streaming  
**Key brief elements:** Official audio links (TikTok + Instagram sound links), content type menu (lip sync, GRWM, travel, dance), artist handles for tagging  
**Creator effort:** LOW–MEDIUM  
**CPM:** $0.50–$2.00  
**Real example:** James Hype "Be Mine", OurHouse/Hï Ibiza

---

### 5. Product Demo Distribution
**Goal:** Drive app downloads, signups, trials through authentic demo content  
**Use when:** Brand has a product people can try and demo on camera or screen-record  
**Key brief elements:** Product access/URL, demo footage OR original UGC instructions, specific CTAs, bot score monitoring  
**Creator effort:** MEDIUM–HIGH — need to understand and authentically demo the product  
**CPM:** $2.00–$10.00  
**Real example:** VitaClips (Santa Cruz Medicinals), TripRank

---

### 6. Personal Brand Growth Engine
**Goal:** Grow an individual person's social following and drive to their main channel  
**Use when:** A person (coach, entrepreneur, podcaster, athlete) wants a creator army redistributing their content  
**Key brief elements:** Dedicated account requirement, subject must always look good, daily content refresh, bio links to main account, impersonation ban guidance  
**Creator effort:** HIGH — dedicated accounts, ongoing daily commitment  
**CPM:** $0.75–$1.50  
**Real example:** Propaganda/DaddyWellness, Yomi Denzel, Carlos Esparraga

---

### 7. Podcast Distribution Network
**Goal:** Grow podcast listeners by distributing episode clips  
**Use when:** Brand runs a podcast and wants short-form clips driving people to full episodes  
**Key brief elements:** Episode content folder, "full episode on YT @handle" required in caption, 80% podcast content rule, no interacting with hosts rule  
**Creator effort:** MEDIUM  
**CPM:** $0.75–$1.50  
**Real example:** DueDiligence Finance podcast

---

### 8. Geo-Targeted Community Builder
**Goal:** Build brand presence in a specific country or region  
**Use when:** Brand needs a specific geographic market (Africa, Canada, Spain, Latin America)  
**Key brief elements:** Geo requirement with enforcement (analytics screenshot mandatory), "how to build [country] audience" guide, posting times for that timezone, local hashtags and location tags  
**Creator effort:** MEDIUM — need the right audience demographics  
**CPM:** $0.50–$1.50  
**Real example:** MarioLopez (Spain), Northstar Bets (Canada), Betway (Nigeria/Ghana)

---

### 9. AI / Slideshow Content Factory
**Goal:** Generate high volume of views with low creator effort  
**Use when:** Brand needs mass reach and is okay with formulaic, high-volume content  
**Key brief elements:** TikTok native slideshow format ONLY (not CapCut exports — this gets rejected), full tool stack guide (Gemini for images, watermark remover), account setup tutorial  
**Creator effort:** LOW — but needs account setup and warmup guide  
**CPM:** $0.75–$1.50  
**Real example:** Reachify/DreamMe (GLP-1 weight loss)

---

### 10. Event & Experience Hype Machine
**Goal:** Drive FOMO and attendance for a live event or recurring experience  
**Use when:** Brand has a recurring event (club night, festival, venue, residency) and wants organic-feeling hype  
**Key brief elements:** Caption bank loaded with FOMO language, POV/GRWM/travel content types, authenticity emphasis ("not forced promotion"), no dedicated account required  
**Creator effort:** LOW–MEDIUM  
**CPM:** $0.50–$2.00  
**Real example:** OurHouse/Hï Ibiza residency

---

## What Separates High vs Low Performing Campaigns

**High performers always have:**
- A pre-written 2-sentence brand story hook (the story IS the content)
- Real example clips with view counts and brief analysis of why they worked
- A specific "never do" list — "no Opus Clip AI subtitles" beats "no AI content"
- Pre-packaged assets (logo PNG, audio file, WeTransfer bundle, Google Drive folder)
- A freshness mechanism (daily new content, updated content bank)
- Caption templates that sound human and native to the platform

**Low performers usually have:**
- A wall of rules with no story or hook
- No example clips provided
- Vague direction ("make engaging content")
- No pre-packaged source material
- No hook or caption templates
- Confusing or unclear payout structure
