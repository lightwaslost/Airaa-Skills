# Hook Library — 70+ Proven Hooks by Angle

Reference this during Phase 6 when writing the Hook Bank section of a brief. Never give generic hooks — always select and adapt from the angles that match the brand category and campaign strategy. Aim for 10–15 hooks in the brief, grouped by angle.

**How to use:** Pick 2–3 angles that fit the brand. Pull 4–5 hooks from each angle. Adapt the bracket placeholders to the specific brand. The closer the hook is to real copy, the better creators perform.

---

## Hook Angle 1: Founder / Origin Story
*Best for: Pre-launch startups, personal brands, consumer products, viral moment campaigns*

These hooks work because people root for underdogs. The origin story IS the hook — let it carry the clip.

- "Two college students built this in their dorm room and just went on Shark Tank"
- "He started this with $0 and [X months] later it's doing [result]"
- "This guy quit his [job] to build something nobody believed in — until now"
- "She had no investors. No team. Just a problem she wanted to fix."
- "This product was rejected [X] times before it went viral"
- "Nobody thought this idea would work. Then it hit [milestone]."
- "He built this because he couldn't find anything like it — so he made it himself"
- "Most people would have quit after [obstacle]. He didn't."

**Real example that worked:** "These two college students built a card that locks your phone apps and went on Shark Tank" — ClipFarm/Bloom. Simple. Specific. No fluff.

---

## Hook Angle 2: Contrarian / Challenger
*Best for: Finance educators, personal brands, SaaS/tech, crypto/Web3, podcasts*

Opens with a belief the viewer holds — then immediately challenges it. Highest retention of any hook type in finance and business content.

- "Nobody tells founders this about raising money"
- "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong"
- "Stop doing [common thing]. Here's what actually works."
- "Most [experts/creators/brands] won't tell you this because it makes them look bad"
- "The reason [popular thing] never works the way people say it does"
- "I've been studying [topic] for [X years]. This is the part nobody talks about."
- "Everyone says [advice]. The data says the opposite."
- "This is the single biggest lie in [industry]"
- "The advice that sounds right but will destroy your [goal]"
- "Why [commonly accepted thing] is actually the worst thing you can do"

**What makes these work:** The viewer CANNOT scroll past because they need to know if their belief is wrong. Use for clips where the guest/host makes a bold, specific claim.

---

## Hook Angle 3: Novelty / "I've Never Seen This Before"
*Best for: Consumer products, travel/event content, personal brand, music*

People stop when they see something they genuinely haven't encountered. Works best when the product or moment is visually interesting.

- "I've never seen anything like this"
- "This exists and I had no idea until now"
- "Wait — you can actually do that?"
- "This is not something I expected to find in [location/category]"
- "I've been doing [thing] for [X years] and just found out there's a better way"
- "This tool does something I thought was impossible"
- "Most people don't know this [place/product/person] exists — it should be everywhere"
- "He showed me something in [country] that genuinely surprised me"

**Real example that worked:** DaddyWellness — "Showing something new — leans into controversy, Americans reacting to something foreign." The novelty IS the hook.

---

## Hook Angle 4: FOMO / "You're Missing Out"
*Best for: Events, venues, music, personal brand travel, pre-launch*

Creates urgency and envy. Best used for experiential content where the viewer isn't there.

- "If you know, you know"
- "POV: you're here and everyone else isn't"
- "I can't believe this exists and more people don't know about it"
- "This is what [place/event] actually looks like on the inside"
- "The [event/venue/experience] you need to put on your list right now"
- "I thought I knew what to expect. I was wrong."
- "There are two types of people: those who've been to [X] and those who haven't — yet"
- "The [city/venue/night] that changed how I think about [music/travel/nightlife]"

---

## Hook Angle 5: Money / Status / Results
*Best for: Finance educators, personal brands, SaaS/tech, crypto/Web3*

Hard numbers beat vague claims every time. Always use specifics when possible.

- "He made [$X] doing [thing most people overlook]"
- "This strategy generates [$X/month] — and it takes [X hours] to set up"
- "Why I stopped [common approach] and switched to this — [result]"
- "The [tool/method] that went from [before metric] to [after metric] in [time]"
- "Most people overpay for [thing]. Here's what they're missing."
- "This is worth [$X]. Most people don't know it's free."
- "The [investment/decision/product] that paid for itself [X] times over"
- "[Specific number] people tried this. Here's what happened."

**Note for crypto:** Avoid "you will make money" or "guaranteed returns" — phrase as results others experienced, not promises. "He turned $X into $Y using [protocol]" is fine. "You'll make [X]%" is not.

## Hook Angle 6: Curiosity / "Wait For It"
*Best for: Podcast clipping, personal brand, product demos, music*

Opens a loop the viewer has to close. Best when combined with a genuine payoff.

- "Wait for it…"
- "Nobody saw this coming"
- "The ending of this clip is not what I expected"
- "Watch what happens at [X seconds]"
- "I've watched this [X] times and still can't believe it"
- "Start watching. You'll understand why in 10 seconds."
- "This conversation took a turn I didn't see coming"
- "He said this out loud on camera and I can't stop thinking about it"
- "She asked him one question. His answer changed everything."

**What makes these work:** The hook creates a question in the viewer's mind. They HAVE to find out the answer. Don't resolve the curiosity in the hook — save it for 10+ seconds in.

---

## Hook Angle 7: Transformation / Before-After
*Best for: Consumer products, health/fitness, SaaS/tech, personal brands*

Shows a journey. Most powerful when the "before" is relatable and the "after" is aspirational.

- "3 months ago I couldn't [problem]. Then I found [product/method]."
- "This is what [X] looks like before vs. after using [product]"
- "I was skeptical. Then I tried it for [X days]. Here's what happened."
- "Most people give up at [stage]. The ones who don't end up like this."
- "The [product/decision] that actually changed [aspect of my life]"
- "I've tried everything for [problem]. This is the first thing that actually worked."
- "Before: [relatable struggle]. After: [specific result]."

**Note for health products:** No specific medical claims. "I noticed X" is fine. "This cures X" is not. Check FDA and FTC guidelines for the specific product category.

---

## Hook Angle 8: Social Proof / Credibility
*Best for: SaaS/tech, crypto/Web3, podcast, finance educators*

Third-party proof removes skepticism faster than any first-person claim.

- "[X] people are already using this — and they're not looking back"
- "This has [X million] views for a reason"
- "[X] creators tried this campaign. Here's the average result."
- "Every serious [niche] person I know uses this tool"
- "The [podcast/creator/brand] that [major media outlet] called 'the most [X] in [category]'"
- "This got [X] reviews in [X days] — all from people who actually used it"
- "He's been doing this for [X years]. The track record speaks for itself."

---

## Hook Angle 9: Relatable / "This Is You"
*Best for: Personal brand, music, consumer products, event hype*

Mirror the viewer's situation back at them. They see themselves in the content.

- "POV: you finally found [thing you've been looking for]"
- "Tell me you [relatable situation] without telling me you [relatable situation]"
- "If you've ever felt like [common struggle] — this is for you"
- "This is what it actually feels like to [experience]"
- "Nobody talks about the [part of topic] that feels like this"
- "Me at [time/situation]: [relatable reaction]"

## Visual Hooks (What Happens on Screen in the First 2 Seconds)

*Separate from caption/voiceover hooks — these are the opening visual moments.*

Most failed clips lose viewers before a word is spoken. The first frame is a still image in the feed — it has to earn the tap.

**For clipping campaigns:**
- Open on the most intense or surprising expression in the clip — not the intro
- Cut straight to the moment, not the build-up
- Zoom in on the speaker's face at a peak-emotion moment as the thumbnail frame
- Never start with someone walking to a chair or introducing themselves

**For music campaigns:**
- First frame should be movement — a turn, a step, a cut to the beat
- Never start on a static pose holding
- Trending: entering a location on the beat drop

**For product demos:**
- Open with the product in use, not the unboxing or setup
- Screen recordings: start on the most impressive output, not the login screen
- "Point of surprise" first — the result, then explain how

**For event/venue content:**
- First frame = best visual moment in the venue (crowd, lights, performer)
- POV works: viewer is in the scene, not watching it
- Never start with exterior shots or arrival footage

**For slideshow campaigns (TikTok native):**
- Slide 1 IS the hook — must stand completely alone as a statement
- Bold text, clean background, maximum contrast
- One idea per slide — don't overload

## Hook Mistakes That Get Clips Rejected

These consistently underperform — do not include in briefs:

- **"Hey guys, today I want to talk about…"** — instant drop-off
- **"So basically…"** — weak opener, no tension
- **"Have you ever wondered…"** — overused, feels like an ad
- **"In this video I'm going to show you…"** — tells them what's coming instead of making them want it
- **Vague hooks:** "This is crazy" / "You won't believe this" with no specifics — builds no real curiosity
- **Too long:** A hook that takes 5 seconds to land loses 60% of viewers before it lands

## How to Mix Angles in a Brief

For most briefs, provide 3 angles with 4–5 hooks each. Structure:

```
## 💡 Hook Bank

**Angle 1 — [Name] (best for [content type]):**
- "[Hook 1]"
- "[Hook 2]"
- "[Hook 3]"
- "[Hook 4]"

**Angle 2 — [Name]:**
- "[Hook 1]"
- "[Hook 2]"
- "[Hook 3]"

**Angle 3 — [Name]:**
- "[Hook 1]"
- "[Hook 2]"
- "[Hook 3]"

*Pick one per video. Don't mix angles in a single clip.*
```

**Angle selection guide by category:**
- Podcaster/YouTube: Curiosity + Contrarian + Social Proof
- Personal Brand: Founder Story + Novelty + FOMO
- Music Artist: FOMO + Relatable + Novelty
- App/SaaS: Transformation + Contrarian + Social Proof
- Crypto/Web3: Contrarian + Money + Social Proof
- Consumer Product: Transformation + Founder Story + Novelty
- Gambling/Logo: (hooks less relevant — visual placement is primary)
- Event/Venue: FOMO + Relatable + Novelty
- Pre-Launch Startup: Founder Story + Curiosity + Contrarian
