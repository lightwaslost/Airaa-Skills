# Brand Categories & Their Natural Campaign Types

Read this during Phase 1 to classify the brand, then use it to pre-select strategies in Phase 2.

The most important insight from analyzing 395 campaigns: **the type of company almost always determines the campaign type**. A podcaster's campaign looks almost nothing like a betting brand's campaign, which looks nothing like a music artist's campaign. Categorize first, then strategize.

---

## Category 1: Podcaster / YouTuber / Video Creator

**Who they are:** Someone who already produces long-form content — podcast episodes, YouTube vlogs, educational videos, interview shows, streams.

**What they almost always do:** Run a **clipping campaign**. They give creators access to their content library and pay per view when creators clip and redistribute it on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

**Why it works:** They have unlimited source material. Creators don't need to film anything — they just download, edit into a 30–60s clip, add captions, and post. Every clip drives viewers back to subscribe to the original channel.

**Typical brief elements:**
- YouTube channel URL or Google Drive folder with episode downloads
- Requirement: bio link must point to their YouTube/Spotify/podcast
- Caption must say "Full episode on YouTube @[handle]" or similar
- Dedicated fan page requirement (some require creators build an account just for this brand)
- Daily or weekly content refresh (new episodes = new clippable material)
- Clips must feature the host in a positive, flattering way

**CPM range:** $0.75–$1.50  
**Creator effort:** Medium-High (dedicated accounts needed for top campaigns)  
**Budget to start:** $1,500–$5,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Has an active YouTube channel (10k+ subs), runs a podcast, does regular video content, mentions "growing their audience" as the goal.

## Category 2: Personal Brand / Coach / Expert / Influencer

**Who they are:** An individual — entrepreneur, investor, fitness coach, lifestyle creator, athlete — building a personal following.

**What they do:** Similar to podcasters, but more personal. They want clips of themselves going viral to grow their social following. Often run campaigns around a "creator army" model where dozens of fan accounts post their best moments.

**Why it works:** Their content is inherently personal and often emotional or motivational. A good quote or insight clips beautifully. The hook practically writes itself.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Subject must always look good, sound good, be portrayed positively — this is non-negotiable
- Dedicated fan page requirement (e.g., "create a page called @[name]fan or @[name]clips")
- "Never post the same clip twice" rule
- Content must drive to their main account (bio link)
- Some require logo or watermark overlay
- Impersonation-related rules (can't pretend to be the person)

**CPM range:** $0.75–$1.50  
**Creator effort:** High (dedicated accounts, ongoing daily posts)  
**Budget to start:** $2,000–$5,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Individual's name is the brand, they have a YouTube/Instagram/TikTok presence, they mention "personal brand" or "growing my following."

## Category 3: Music Artist / Record Label / DJ

**Who they are:** An artist releasing a song, an album, or building a fanbase. Could be a DJ, singer, rapper, producer, or label representing multiple artists.

**What they do:** Run a **music promotion campaign** — two sub-types:
- **Audio attach (most common):** Creators use the official TikTok/Instagram sound in their own content. The creator makes whatever content they want, they just use the song as the soundtrack. Very low friction.
- **Artist clipping:** Creators clip existing music videos, live performances, or behind-the-scenes content and post it.

**Why it works:** Music is inherently emotional. When a sound goes viral on TikTok, it can hit hundreds of millions of plays. Audio attach campaigns also benefit from the TikTok algorithm surfacing the sound organically.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Official TikTok sound link + Instagram audio link (both required)
- Content type menu: lip sync, GRWM, travel, dancing, lifestyle, cooking, gym — very open
- Artist handles for tagging (@artist on TikTok and Instagram)
- Streaming links (Spotify, Apple Music) in bio
- Example content links (real creators using the sound well)
- "Use the song naturally, not forced" framing — authenticity matters

**CPM range:** $0.50–$2.00  
**Creator effort:** Low (audio attach) to Medium (artist clipping)  
**Budget to start:** $1,000–$3,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Brand is an artist/musician, references a song or album, has a TikTok sound or music video, wants "streams" or "chart position."

## Category 4: App / SaaS / Tech Product

**Who they are:** A software product — mobile app, web platform, AI tool, productivity tool, B2B SaaS.

**What they do:** Run a **product demo UGC campaign**. Creators get access to the product (free trial, promo code, or it's free) and make authentic screen-recording or reaction content showing themselves using it.

**Why it works:** "Look at this tool I found" is one of the most trusted content formats on TikTok and Instagram. Seeing a real person use a product removes skepticism. The key is authenticity — creators who genuinely engage with the product perform far better than those just reading a script.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Product access URL (where creators sign up or download)
- Screen recording requirement or "demo in frame" instruction
- Specific CTA: download link in bio, "try it free at [URL]"
- Hook angle (what's surprising or counterintuitive about the product)
- Bot score monitoring often mentioned (ContentRewards watches for fake engagement on these)
- Sometimes a promo code for discount

**CPM range:** $2.00–$10.00  
**Creator effort:** Medium-High (need to understand and demo the product)  
**Budget to start:** $3,000–$10,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Mentions an app, software, platform, tool, SaaS, "try our product," downloads, or signups as the goal.

## Category 5: Crypto / Web3 / DeFi / Finance Protocol

**Who they are:** Blockchain projects, DeFi protocols, crypto exchanges, NFT platforms, prediction markets, DAOs.

**What they do:** Similar to SaaS — but content leans more toward **explainer UGC** than product demos. The concept itself is the hook. Creators explain what the protocol does, why it's different, and why they should sign up.

**Unique challenges:** Crypto content is restricted on many platforms. FTC and platform rules around financial promotions apply. Creators must not make specific financial claims.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Concept explanation: what the product does in plain English (the brief must do this work for the creator)
- Hook angle: the most counterintuitive or surprising thing about the protocol
- Explicit prohibition on financial advice language ("you will make money," "guaranteed returns")
- No competitor mentions (Polymarket, Uniswap, etc.)
- Sometimes KYC/signup flow needs to be explained in the brief
- Celestia, Ethereum, Solana ecosystem notes if relevant

**CPM range:** $5.00–$25.00  
**Creator effort:** High (need to understand the concept well enough to explain it)  
**Budget to start:** $5,000–$15,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Mentions blockchain, crypto, DeFi, Web3, NFTs, tokens, protocol, DAO, on-chain.

## Category 6: Consumer Product / Physical Goods / E-commerce

**Who they are:** A physical product brand — supplements, skincare, food/beverage, apparel, gadgets, beauty products.

**What they do:** Usually a **product demo or unboxing UGC campaign**. Creators receive or purchase the product and make content featuring it — reviews, before/afters, tutorials, taste tests.

**Viral moment variant:** If the brand has an interesting story (Shark Tank appearance, founder story, viral press), they can run a Viral Moment Amplifier with that story as the hook.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Product purchase link or promo code (or brand ships product directly)
- Content types: unboxing, taste test, gym use, morning routine, before/after
- Specific health claims to avoid (FDA compliance)
- Brand handle for tagging
- Hook angle: what makes this product different or surprising
- Aspirational framing encouraged

**CPM range:** $1.00–$5.00  
**Creator effort:** Medium (need to actually use the product)  
**Budget to start:** $2,000–$8,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Sells a physical product, mentions "try our product," "unboxing," reviews, or has an e-commerce store.

## Category 7: Gambling / Sports Betting / Casino

**Who they are:** Sports betting platforms, online casinos, fantasy sports apps. Heavily regulated.

**What they do:** Almost exclusively **logo placement campaigns**. They give creators a logo PNG and pay them to overlay it on sports content. Very low creative requirement — just place the logo visibly on any sports highlight or meme.

**Why:** Regulations make explicit product promotion difficult. Logo placement builds brand awareness without making claims.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Logo file (PNG with transparent background, usually multiple sizes/colors)
- Geo requirement (very strict — South Africa, Canada, Nigeria, specific countries only)
- Strict prohibited content list, especially regarding minors — this is legal compliance, not just brand safety
- Sports content only: soccer, basketball, tennis, hockey, football, baseball, F1
- No alcohol, drugs, or NSFW content anywhere near the logo
- Competitors explicitly banned

**CPM range:** $0.30–$1.00  
**Creator effort:** Very Low  
**Budget to start:** $1,000–$5,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Sports betting, casino, gambling, "sports book," mentions specific sports leagues.

## Category 8: Event / Venue / Club / Festival

**Who they are:** A recurring live event, nightclub, music venue, festival, sports team, or recurring experience brand.

**What they do:** Run an **event hype campaign** — drive FOMO and attendance. Content feels organic, not promotional. Creators post POV videos, GRWMs before attending, travel vlogs, or "you need to experience this" content.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Caption bank loaded with FOMO language ("if you know, you know," "the Wednesday standard," etc.)
- Authenticity emphasis: content should feel like a genuine fan, not a sponsor
- POV, GRWM, travel, lifestyle content types
- No "this is an ad" framing
- Event dates/schedule if time-sensitive

**CPM range:** $0.50–$2.00  
**Creator effort:** Low-Medium  
**Budget to start:** $1,000–$3,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** Mentions a club, venue, festival, "event," recurring dates, nightlife, entertainment experience.

---

## Category 9: Pre-Launch Startup

**Who they are:** A company that hasn't launched publicly yet, or just launched — building buzz before or around a launch moment.

**What they do:** **Viral Moment Amplifier** — the launch itself is the "moment." Content focuses on the concept and drives to a waitlist or early signup.

**Key difference from SaaS:** Creators often can't demo the product yet. Content is concept-driven ("this platform is about to change X") rather than product-driven.

**Typical brief elements:**
- Concept hook — the most interesting or surprising thing about what's coming
- Waitlist link or early access URL as the CTA
- No claims about features that don't exist yet
- Urgency framing if there's a launch date

**CPM range:** $0.50–$2.00  
**Creator effort:** Low (concept content, no product needed)  
**Budget to start:** $1,000–$5,000  

**Signs this is the right category:** "Launching soon," waitlist, early access, beta, mentions a launch date, or the product isn't live yet.

---

## Category 10: Finance / Investment Educator (Not a product)

**Who they are:** A finance educator, stock analyst, real estate investor, or wealth educator with YouTube, podcast, or newsletter.

**What they do:** Similar to podcasters — **clipping campaign** to drive YouTube subscribers or podcast listeners.

**Unique considerations:** Finance content is heavily regulated on some platforms. No specific investment advice in clips. Creator must have appropriate content niche (finance TikTok, "FinTok").

**Typical brief elements:**
- YouTube channel with educational investing content
- "FinTok" niche preferred (creator page should be finance/investing focused)
- Explicit prohibition on investment advice in clips
- Drive to YouTube via bio link
- Clips should feature the educator looking credible and expert

**CPM range:** $0.75–$1.50  
**Budget to start:** $2,000–$5,000

## Quick Classification Guide

| Signal in brand/URL | Most likely category | Airaa Campaign Type |
|---------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| YouTube channel, podcast | Podcaster / Creator | Clipping |
| Personal name is the brand | Personal Brand | Clipping |
| Music, artist name, "song" | Music Artist | Instant Tasks + UGC |
| App, software, tool, AI | App / SaaS | UGC |
| Blockchain, crypto, DeFi, Web3 | Crypto / Web3 | UGC |
| Physical product, "buy now" store | Consumer Product | UGC |
| Betting, casino, sports book | Gambling | UGC or Clipping (logo overlay) |
| Club, venue, festival, event | Event | Instant Tasks + UGC |
| "Launching," waitlist, beta | Pre-Launch Startup | Instant Tasks + UGC |
| Finance educator, "investing" | Finance Educator | Clipping |
